Aerodynamic Forces

  • The primary forces that act on an airplane are:
    1. Thrust
    2. Drag
    3. Lift
    4. Weight
  • Thrust is the force that propels the airplane forward, usually provided by engines.
  • Drag is the force that acts against thrust, and it pushes the plane on the opposite direction to it's movement.
  • Lift is the force that keeps the airplane in the air, usually provided by the wings.
  • Weight is the force that pushes the airplane in the downwards direction, and is a result of gravity.

Changing the Forces

  • These forces can be manipulated by changing the shape of the airplane.
  • Control surfaces like elevators and rudders can be used to redirect the flow of air around the plane, which could be used to make it change direction.
  • Elevators are usually located on the wings of a plane, and move up and down to control the flow of air to steer the airplane up and down.
  • Rudders are usually located on the back of a plane, and move left and right to control the flow of air to steer the airplane left and right.
  • The bigger these surfaces are, the more the direction of the airplane can be controlled.
